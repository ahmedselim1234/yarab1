SUPRA

The beginning of the Toyota Supra goes back to 1978 when Toyota launched it as a model of its cars. The Supra had simple beginnings in the world of sports cars, but it quickly became one of the most famous and popular Japanese sports cars in the world. Toyota Supra was famous for its excellent performance and attractive design, and it always competed strongly in the medium- and high-performance sports car category. It is worth noting that the Supra was very popular in the modified car enthusiast community and was widely supported by a wide range of parts and accessory manufacturers.

Car Generations

The Toyota Supra has seen several different generations since its debut in 1978, and here is an overview of the main generations: 

*First generation (A40/A50):
-Production: 1978-1981
-2.0 liter engines
-Power ranges from 110 to 123 horsepower
-Available in hatchback and coupe styles

*Second generation (A60):
-Production: 1981-1986
-Engines with a capacity of 2.8 liters
-Power ranges from 145 to 160 horsepower
-Design and performance improvements
*Third generation (A70):
-Production: 1986-1993
-3.0 liter engines
-Power ranges from 200 to 230 horsepower
-More dynamic design and powerful performance
